# Context — `sst-cam-app`

**What it is.** The companion mobile app (Flutter). In the SST-Cam architecture
it is the **single source of truth**: it owns all business data — users, teams,
rosters, match configs, streaming destinations, and the overlay design. It is
the only initiator on the BLE control channel.

**How it relates to us.** This firmware repo is a **stateless executor** of what
the app pushes. The app sends a session config once per session, then pushes
events (recording control, match events, score updates, overlay layout); the
firmware reacts and produces files/streams. The firmware never persists business
data across sessions and never pushes unsolicited data — the app polls
(telemetry ~1 Hz). Both sides share the `sst-cam-proto` wire contract.

**Standing assumptions.**
- The app is always the initiator; firmware always responds (exactly one
  `CommandResponse` per `Command`, matched by `correlation_id`).
- The app discovers the camera via a two-layer scan filter: the advertised
  service UUID **and** the `sst-cam-NNNN` device-name prefix (U5).
- The app drives the session lifecycle ordering: connect → `GetDeviceInfo` →
  `StartWifiDirect` → `PushSessionConfig` → `PushOverlayLayout` → record/stream/
  events (U6).
- WiFi Direct credentials are **generated by the camera** per session and
  reported back over BLE; the app consumes whatever the camera generated
  (see `outbound/2026-06-08-wifi-direct-dynamic-credentials.md`).
- Overlay pixel-parity between the app's Flutter preview and the camera's
  Cairo/Pango render depends on shared fonts + agreed coordinate/anchor
  semantics (see `outbound/2026-06-08-overlay-pixel-parity-contract.md`).
